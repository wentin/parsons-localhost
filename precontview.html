PRE-COLLEGE AND CONTINUING STUDIES

Parsons Summer, Pre-College Academy, and Continuing Education programs are housed together as Parsons SPACE. The SPACE programs offer open enrollment, nondegree study options in art and design for students of all ages and levels who want to take courses for credit or noncredit. Parsons SPACE classes are available in a variety of formats, settings, and schedules.

SUMMER PROGRAMS

High school, college, and adult students can choose from two and four-week Summer Intensive Seminars (SIS) in New York City or Paris. Students interested in Architecture, Interior, and Lighting Design can enroll in the five-week Summer Studies in Constructed Environments at Parsonsâ€™ New York campus. Graduate students can join the two-week Graduate International Summer Program in Berlin offered by the Masters Program in the History of Decorative Arts. In addition, arts educators can spend three summers pursuing a Masters of Science in Education through the Leadership in the Arts graduate degree program, which is offered in cooperation with the Bank Street College of Education in New York.

PRE-COLLEGE ACADEMY

Providing diverse classes and certificate options for students in grades 4 through 12, Parsons Pre-College Academy meets Saturdays during the school year and for two weeks during the summer. Age appropriate courses range from fashion, graphic, and interior design to drawing, painting, cartooning, photography, and portfolio development.

CONTINUING EDUCATION

Open admission, nondegree studio, history, and business-oriented courses in art and design for beginner adults and accomplished professionals are available through Parsons Continuing Education. Offered each semester, classes provide students with the technical skills and design training they need to begin or advance their careers and develop their artistic interests. Several courses are available online. Students may enroll in Parsons Continuing Education classes for credit or noncredit, or apply courses towards earning a Parsons SPACE certificate.
